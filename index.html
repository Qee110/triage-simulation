Simple guide

The case study uses a few technical terms. This guide keeps the meaning simple.

ESI

Priority level. Lower number means the patient is more urgent.

Severity

A score from 0 to 10 used to estimate how serious the condition is.

Utility

A fit score. Higher value means the unit suits the patient better.

SoftMax chance

The chance that each unit is chosen after comparing all utility values.

ESI mapping

This follows the severity ranges shown in the case study.

Severity 8 and above

ESI 1 to 2, critical, usually ICU.

Severity 6 to below 8

ESI 2 to 3, high urgency, ICU or ER.

Severity 4 to below 6

ESI 3, moderate, usually ER.

Severity 2 to below 4

ESI 4, low urgency, usually Fast Track.

Severity below 2

ESI 5, minor, usually Fast Track.

Formulas used

These are the same model ideas from the assignment, written in a short and readable way.

Utility

Utility = minus the distance between patient severity and unit target, minus waiting time weight times current wait.

Probability

Chance for a unit = exp(utility) divided by the sum of exp(utility) for all units.

New waiting time

New wait = current wait + max(0, assigned patients minus capacity) times congestion effect.

New severity

New severity = old severity + waiting time times worsening rate.
